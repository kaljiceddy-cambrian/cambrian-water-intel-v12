15. Methodology & Assumptions

Industrial surcharge program: Utility levies extra charges when wastewater exceeds 'normal' strength (typically ~250 mg/L BOD and ~250‚Äì350 mg/L TSS)

High-strength industries face recurring penalties ‚Üí strong ROI wedge for pretreatment/reuse

Surcharge details present: Specific rates/thresholds were found in public documents

Enables precise '$ saved per lb removed' ROI modeling in outreach

Combined bill examples: Illustrative bills assume 50,000 gal/month (except Phoenix where base includes first 3,740 gal)

Comparable across markets; use for CFO-level 'monthly pain' framing

Data confidence note: Lower where industrial-class rates were unavailable and a residential proxy was used

Treat as 'qualify + verify' before quoting hard numbers

Normalization: All rates normalized to $/1,000 gallons for comparability

Supports apples-to-apples ranking and targeting

Unit conversion: 1 CCF = 748 gallons

Reconcile utility docs (often in CCF) with $/kgal tables

Key Assumptions
  • All rates normalized to $/1,000 gallons (1 CCF = 748 gallons).
  • Combined bills assume 50,000 gal/month industrial usage.
  • Lower-confidence entries use residential proxies.
  • Regulatory data reflects published state agency documents as of Feb 2026.
