1. What Is This Data?

The Cambrian NPDES Leads Pipeline is a curated database of 44,594 qualified US industrial wastewater facilities (filtered from a raw universe of 1,148,393 NPDES/TRI records) that have been scored, ranked, and enriched specifically for Cambrian sales outreach. Every facility holds an EPA NPDES permit and/or files Toxics Release Inventory (TRI) reports — meaning they are potential customers for Cambrian's treatment technology.

The data combines 9 federal and commercial data sources, verified market intelligence, and proprietary scoring to answer one question: "Which facilities have the strongest business case for Cambrian's treatment system?"

The pipeline exports 197 columns per facility across 34 segment CSVs (20 territory/master segments + 14 BDR action lists). Every single facility has a pre-built bdr_call_script — a Sandler-methodology research brief with facility context, contacts, compliance signals, and discovery questions ready for outreach.

Why should you trust this data? Every record traces back to official EPA permit, violation, inspection, and enforcement data. Flow numbers come from actual Discharge Monitoring Reports filed with the EPA. Market rates are researcher-verified from 148 utility districts across 42 states. Scores are formula-driven, not subjective. V12 adds FRS NAICS/SIC codes, mailing addresses, enforcement dollar breakdowns, and alternative facility names.

8. CapEx Qualification — Does the Math Work?

Every facility is evaluated on whether Cambrian's treatment system makes financial sense — not just technical sense. This is the CapEx qualification gate.

What Is "Implied Growth CapEx"?

The implied_growth_capex field answers: "How much annual economic value is flowing through this facility's wastewater system?"

Plain English: If a facility's implied growth CapEx is $11M, it means: "Based on how much water this plant discharges and what water costs in their market, there is roughly $11M of annual economic value at play — enough to justify a $7.5M+ treatment system with strong payback."

The Formula

Implied Growth CapEx = Flow (gal/day) × 365 days × Blended Rate ($/gal) × EBITDA Margin (60%) × IRR Multiple (7.0x)
InputValueWhat It Means
Flow (gal/day) from data Best available daily wastewater discharge, converted from MGD to gallons.
× 365 365 Annualizes the daily flow.
× Blended Rate ($/gal) from data Combined water + sewer cost per gallon from verified 148-utility-district database.
× EBITDA Margin 60% Conservative industry benchmark for treatment provider operating margin.
× IRR Multiple 7.0x Conservative internal rate of return multiplier. Typical water infrastructure range: 7-12x.

Worked Example: Meat Processing Plant in Iowa

StepCalculation
Flow: 0.8 MGD0.8 × 1,000,000 = 800,000 gal/day
Annualize800,000 × 365 = 292,000,000 gal/year
Blended rate: $0.013/gal (Iowa market)292,000,000 × $0.013 = $3,796,000/year
EBITDA margin: 60%$3,796,000 × 0.60 = $2,277,600/year
IRR multiple: 7.0x$2,277,600 × 7.0 = $15,943,200
Implied Growth CapEx$15.9M — well above $7.5M threshold → QUALIFIED

10. Sandler Outreach Signals — Confirm Reality, Uncover Pain

Every signal below gives you a reason to call. Use the Micro-UFC opener for every first touch, then pivot to the signal-specific observation.

Micro-UFC Opener (use for every signal):
"Quick one — I'm reaching out because I noticed something in public records; if it's not relevant, we can drop it — fair?"
This sets the Up-Front Contract: gives them an out, lowers their guard, and earns permission to share the observation.
SignalFieldWhy It MattersSandler OpeningPain Funnel Starter
SNC Flag snc_flag = 1 Public compliance exposure; creates internal pressure + cost "I might be off, but I'm seeing your site listed as SNC in public data. Is compliance currently a focus, or is that already resolved?" "If it is a focus, what's the practical impact right now — time, cost, leadership attention, risk?"
Permit Expiring permit_expiring_12m_flag = 1 Renewal triggers review, engineering, timelines "Looks like your permit renewal window is coming up within ~12 months. Are you already set on the renewal plan, or still assessing?" "What part of renewal is most painful — sampling, limits, engineering, schedule, or internal alignment?"
Enforcement active_federal_case_flag = 1 Formal action drives urgency + change mandates "I noticed prior enforcement activity in the record. Is that behind you, or does it still affect how the team operates?" "What changed after that — process, oversight, cost, reporting burden?"
Hot Zone hot_zone_flag = 1 Converging stressors raise cost shock probability "You're in an area where rates/regulatory pressure tend to tighten. Are water/sewer economics becoming a board-level topic?" "If rates keep moving the way they've been, what breaks first — margin, production, expansion, or compliance?"
CapEx Qualified capex_qualification_status = QUALIFIED Model indicates payback potential "We ran a rough model using public inputs — it suggests there may be an economic case for process changes. Does your team look at water/sewer as a controllable cost?" "If you could reduce total water/sewer cost meaningfully, where would that show up — budget, expansion, risk?"

BDR Call Script Column Every Facility

The bdr_call_script column is a pre-generated Sandler-methodology natural language brief per facility. Every qualified facility (44,594) has a call script. It includes:

  • Facility context (name, location, owner, flow, contaminants, treatment type)
  • Named contacts with emails from FRS and TRI
  • Key compliance signals with specific dollar amounts
  • 4-6 tailored discovery questions based on the facility's specific signals
  • Domain warnings (third-party operator flags, stale contact indicators)
Example: Molson Coors Golden Brewery

MOLSON COORS USA LLC -- GOLDEN BREWERY is a food facility located at 12TH AND FORD STS, GOLDEN, CO 80401. It is owned by ROBERT MARUYAMA. The facility discharges approximately 13.00 MGD. It is regulated for nutrients, BOD/COD/TSS, metals, and toxicity parameters. The inferred treatment technology is advanced biological (confidence: medium-high). The following individuals are listed as points of contact: JEREMY WOOLF per EPA FRS records; and JOSH NOEL (JOSH.NOEL@MOLSONCOORS.COM) as the TRI reporting contact.

This facility has an active federal enforcement case with $4,254,900 in assessed penalties. The area has extremely high water stress (Aqueduct BWS: 4.17/5). The facility's NPDES permit expires in 8 months — a natural upgrade window.

Discovery questions: (1) I saw some public compliance data that caught my eye — is that something you've been working through? (2) With the permit renewal window coming up, have you started looking at treatment technology options? (3) At 13 MGD, what percentage of your operational costs comes from water and wastewater? (4) If water allocations got cut 30% in your area, what would that do to production?

Sandler Rule: Never make claims about their situation — you are working from public data, not inside knowledge. Always give them an out: "I might be off...", "Is that already resolved?". If they confirm the pain, go deeper with the Pain Funnel.

14. Sandler BDR Playbook — Using the Data

This playbook follows the Sandler Selling System. You are not pitching Cambrian — you are using public data signals to start honest conversations about whether there is a real problem worth solving.

Step 1: Build Your Hit List

Quick start filter: territory = [your territory] + gate = 1 + contact_complete_flag = 1
Or: pick a BDR action list from Section 12 that matches your outreach angle.

Step 2: Read the Call Script (30 seconds)

Open the bdr_call_script column for your target facility. It contains:

  1. Facility context (name, location, owner, flow, contaminants)
  2. Named contacts with emails
  3. Key compliance signals with dollar amounts
  4. 4-6 tailored discovery questions

Step 3: Pick Your Signal (One Per Lead)

Priority order:

  1. SNC-flagged + CapEx Qualified — Highest urgency + economics work.
  2. Permit Expiring (< 12 months) — Time-sensitive upgrade window.
  3. Recent Enforcement + Penalties — Past pain, open to preventing recurrence.
  4. Hot Zone + POTW Dependent — Cost pressure rising.
  5. Violations (recurring) — Process drift headache.
  6. P2 / Source Reduction Activity — Openness signal.
  7. CapEx Qualified (no other signal) — Pure economics, soft opener.

Step 4: The Sandler Call Framework

1
Micro-UFC (Up-Front Contract)
"Quick one — I'm reaching out because I noticed something in public records; if it's not relevant, we can drop it — fair?"
↓
2
Signal-Specific Observation
State what you see — as a question, not a claim. (See Section 10 for scripts.)
↓
3
Pain Funnel (if they confirm)
"What's the practical impact?" → "How long?" → "What have you tried?" → "How much is it costing?" → "Have you given up solving it?"
↓
4
Next Step (only if pain is real)
"Would it make sense to have a 20-minute call where we share what we've seen with similar facilities?"

Step 5: Multi-Site Account Plays

Use bdr_enterprise_targets (24,595 facilities) or bdr_multi_site_parent (4,056 facilities) to identify multi-site operators.

Enterprise UFC: "We work with companies that have multiple facilities with wastewater permits. I can see from public data that [Company] has [X] sites across [Y] states. We built a model that flags which sites might have the strongest economic case — would it be worth 15 minutes to see if any of it resonates?"

What NOT to Do

Sandler Anti-Patterns:
  • Don't lead with Cambrian's capabilities. They care what their problem costs them.
  • Don't cite the implied_growth_capex dollar amount. It is a model input.
  • Don't stack signals. Pick one. Go deep.
  • Don't assume the data is current. Frame as: "I'm seeing..."
  • Don't chase. If they say it is not relevant, say "Fair enough." Move on. The list has 14,763 Gate 1 leads.

15. FAQ

General

Q: How fresh is this data?

The pipeline was last run in February 2026 (V12). EPA data sources are updated quarterly. Market rates were researcher-verified in early 2026. Violation and enforcement data covers the trailing 24-60 months depending on the metric.

Q: Why are there 1.1M rows in DuckDB but only ~252K in the CSVs?

The export applies quality filters that remove sewerage systems, excluded industries (municipal water, electric utilities without wastewater), terminated facilities, and facilities without NPDES permits or TRI records. The ~252K rows are the qualified prospect universe.

Q: What is the difference between "leads_master_fb" and "leads_T1_west_fb"?

leads_master_fb.csv is ALL F&B facilities nationwide (26,614 rows). leads_T1_west_fb.csv is the F&B subset for the West territory only (7,018 rows). Same 197 columns, just filtered by territory.

Q: What are the new V12 columns?

V12 added 16 columns: FRS NAICS/SIC codes (frs_naics_code, frs_naics_desc, frs_sic_code, frs_sic_desc), mailing addresses (frs_mailing_address/city/state/zip), enforcement dollar breakdowns (case_fed_penalty_amt, case_state_penalty_amt, case_sep_cost_amt, case_compliance_cost_amt), alternative names (frs_alternative_names), and FRS National Single data (frs_interest_types, frs_pgm_sys_acrnms, us_mexico_border_ind).

Columns

Q: What is frs_alternative_names?

DBA (Doing Business As) and alias names for the facility from EPA's FRS registry. Useful for matching facilities that operate under different names. Example: a Tyson plant might also be registered as "IBP Fresh Meats."

Q: What is us_mexico_border_ind?

A flag indicating the facility is within 100km of the US-Mexico border. Comes from the FRS National Single file. Relevant for border region environmental compliance programs and bilateral water management initiatives.

Q: What is the bdr_call_script?

A pre-generated Sandler-methodology natural language research brief for every qualified facility (44,594 total — 100% coverage). It includes facility context, named contacts with emails, compliance signals with dollar amounts, and 4-6 tailored discovery questions. See Section 10 for the Molson Coors example.

Q: What if a facility is already in HubSpot?

The hubspot_active_deal_flag column will be 1. Check hubspot_deal_name, hubspot_deal_stage, and hubspot_deal_owner to see who is working it. If the deal is stale (no activity in 60+ days), coordinate with the deal owner. If it is actively being worked, skip it.

Q: How do I use case_fed_penalty_amt vs case_total_penalties_usd?

case_total_penalties_usd is the grand total. V12 breaks this into: case_fed_penalty_amt (federal), case_state_penalty_amt (state), case_sep_cost_amt (SEP costs), case_compliance_cost_amt (compliance actions). Use breakdowns for specific talking points.

Scoring

Q: Can a facility have Gate 1 but NOT be CapEx Qualified?

Yes. Gate is based on lead_score_0_100 (compliance pressure, industry fit, flow, market signals). CapEx qualification only checks economics at local water rates. A facility with severe SNC and high flow but low rates could be Gate 1 but not CapEx Qualified.

Q: What does "flow confidence" mean?

flow_confidence_flag: high (from ICIS actual or DMR average), medium (permit design flow), low (outfall or enrichment), none (no flow data).

Workflow

Q: The call script contacts seem outdated — where do I find current contacts?

Three backup options:

  1. FRS Facility Detail page: https://ofmpub.epa.gov/frs_public2/fii_query_detail.disp_program_facility?p_registry_id={frs_registry_id}
  2. EPA ECHO: https://echo.epa.gov/detailed-facility-report?fid={npdes_id}
  3. ZoomInfo/Apollo: Search parent_company_resolved + city for fresh contacts

Q: Can I see this facility's actual permit?

Yes — go to echo.epa.gov and search by the npdes_id. You will see the full permit, inspection history, and compliance record.

Q: What is a "hot zone"?

Hot zones are geographic areas where water stress, regulatory stringency, and high water/sewer costs converge. The hot_zone_tier field rates intensity (1 = highest), and hot_zone_gtm_play gives you the recommended sales approach. 619,085 facilities are in hot zones.
